Pretreating raw FTIR spectra - smoothing + 2nd derivatives

The raw FTIR spectra were firstly smoothed using Savitzky-Golay filter (width = 7, poly = 2). And second derivatives were calculated from smoothed spectra (in EMSC::SavitzkyGolay function, deriv = 2). Only signature area (800 - 1800 cm-1) was used in following analysis. The pre-treated spectra looked like:

Input GCMS signals

UAC GCMS signals were input and merged with FTIR spectra according to their batch information

PLS analysis between FTIR spectra and GCMS signals

In this section, the GCMS signals were input into PLS model as response variable, and pretreated FTIR spectral signals were input as independent variables. The decision on the number of component is made according to the accumulated variance explained by components, and the number is selected when >85% variance is explained.

Overviews on GC-MS signals and FTIR PLS fitted values

GC-MS signals and PLS fitted values from FTIR spectra were shown in box plots, and were further sub-divided according to UV and Control groups.

Malformation rates of pollen grains

Counting results on malformation rate were input and merged with GC-MS and FTIR signals. An independent figure were plotted here to show the variations of malformation rates among donor plants.

Donor plant comparison - mean and variance of malformation rates/GCMS/FTIR signals

The malformation rate indicates plant’s stability of mitosis process, including both its mean and sd. The GCMS/FTIR signals indicate the quantification of UAC within pollen grains, positively correlated to screening ability towards UV-B radiation. This section aims at exploring the relationship between malformation rate and GCMS/FTIR signals, by looking at their means and variances, with assumption that accumulation of UAC may contribute to more stable mitosis process.
